# block-riot
A lightweight browser extension that automatically filters and blocks everything about Riot Games, from your Google & DuckDuckGo search results. Keep your search results clean and focused on what matters to you. It may be block everything related terms and common misspellings.

## Privacy Policy

### 1. Introduction

The Riot Games Content Blocker browser extension ("the Extension") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what information the Extension does and does not collect, and how it operates.


### 2. No Data Collection

The Extension does NOT collect, store, transmit, or share any personal data, browsing history, or any other information. Specifically:

   1.  No Personal Information Collected: We do not collect your name, email address, location, IP address, or any personally identifiable information.
   2.   No Browsing History: We do not track or store your browsing history, search queries, or visited websites.
   3.   No Content Data: We do not collect, store, or transmit any content from web pages you visit.
   4.   No Analytics: We do not use analytics tools, tracking pixels, or any monitoring software.
   5.   No Third-Party Sharing: Since we collect no data, there is no data to share with third parties.


### 3. How the Extension Works

The Extension operates entirely locally within your browser:

1. Local Processing: All filtering and content analysis happens locally on your device.
2. No External Communication: The Extension does not connect to any external servers or services.
3. No Data Transmission: No information leaves your browser or device.

### 4. Content Filtering Process

The Extension works by:

1. Scanning Google search results pages for specific text patterns related to Riot Games, Valorant, and League of Legends.
2. Applying visual overlays to matching content to indicate it has been filtered.
3. All processing occurs in real-time within your browser and is immediately discarded after processing.

### 5. Open Source

The Extension's source code is publicly available at [here](https://github.com/leidorf/block-riot), allowing anyone to verify that no data collection occurs.
